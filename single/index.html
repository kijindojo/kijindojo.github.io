Genbukan Kijin Dojo UK
Welcome to the Kijin Dojo website.

Kijin Dojo is dedicated to following the martial art traditions and techniques prescribed by Grandmaster Shoto Tanemura and is an official Dojo of the Genbukan World Ninpo Bugei Federation.

Classes are held in Reading and new members are always welcome, see the training section for more details.

Grandmaster

Grandmaster Tanemura was born with the name Tsunehisa Tanemura on the 28th of August 1947, in the town of Matsubushi, which is located in Saitama prefecture, Japan. Tsunehisa was his given name, but later he took the name "Shoto" meaning the "Law of the Sword".

Tanemura Sensei's martial arts training began with his father when he was just 9 years old, training in both unarmed and armed techniques. He continued to study martial arts with dedication and was awarded his first Menkyo Kaiden (full mastership) in Shinden Fudo Ryu and Kukishin Ryu at the age of twenty. He has continued to study and teach martial arts, establishing the Genbukan World Ninpo Bugei in 1984 to show to the world what a true Japanese martial art is.

Currently Grandmaster Tanemura is the president of three organisations and holds masterships in over twenty schools.

Genbukan

The Genbukan was established on November 28th, 1984 by Grandmaster. The name 'Genbukan' means the place that nurtures the martial art professionals (Kuroto): the place radiating with an exquisite martial art. 'Gen' also means black - a place of practice for techniques which are mysterious, strange and marvellous. The significance is that the one who masters the Ninpo Bugei gives light to the (outer) world of darkness.

The Symbolism of the Genbukan Emblem

The Buddist indomitable sacred wheel, the eight-spoked Chakra (Horin), is the symbol of the Genbukan. The Chakra was originally an ancient weapon of India, later adopted for rituals in Tantric Buddhism. It symbolises the destruction of evil andall that comes in the way of Dharma, which is the law and order of the universe. In the centre is a sacred mirror manifesting the oneness of the divine spirit and soul. With the character for perseverance 'Nin' is expressed the desire to attain this oneness.

The Lotus petals, surrounding the mirror, signify that however 'muddy' the environment is we keep our hearts pure and blossom out of the dirt and mire.
With sacred swords facing the eight cardinal directions evil cannot find its way to cause harm against us. Believing that ultimately the martial way and the way of Buddha is one and the same Grandmaster established this emblem for the Genbukan to emanate the infinite light of the noble wheel.

Origins of the Genbukan

The Genbukan was created from many schools; some more than 1000 years old, by Grandmaster Tanemura, the present World Ninpo Bugei Federation President. Genbukan Ninpo is not limited to any one style (Ryu) the Genbukan basics come from various Ryu ha, including:

  • Togakure Ryu
  • Kumogakure Ryu
  • Kukishin Ryu
  • Gyokko Ryu
  • Gyokushin Ryu
  • Koto Ryu
  • Takagi Ryu
  • Gikan Ryu

Within these Ryu you can find eighteen traditional topics of study, the "Bugei Juhappan". There have been many different versions of the bugei juhappan, reflecting the differing needs of the time. The following list has been suggested as a fairly stable core account.

  1. Taijutsu Unarmed fighting
  2. Kenjutsu Sword techniques
  3. Bojutsu Staff fighting
  4. Shurikenjutsu Blade throwing techniques
  5. Kyujutsu Archery
  6. Sojutsu Spear techniques
  7. Naginatajutsu Halberd fighting
  8. Kusarigamajutsu Sickle and chain
  9. Hensojutsu Disguises
  10. Shinobi iri Stealth and infiltration techniques
  11. Suiren Swimming
  12. Bo ryaku Strategy
  13. Juttejutsu Iron rod techniques
  14. Intonjutsu Escape and evasion
  15. Tenmon Meteorology
  16. Chimon Geography
  17. Bajutsu Horsemanship
  18. Hojojutsu Tying with ropes

These were the main subjects that a ninja had to master, but there were two that remained as the most important: 'Seishinteki Kyoyo' (Spiritual refinement), which involves the heart, spirit and soul; and Taijutsu (Unarmed defence), involving the body and the physical techniques. Both combined produce miraculous powers and gives the practitioner the 'Shin Gi Tai Icchi' (Heart, technique and body combination).

Instructor

Laura Nakano started studying Judo at the age of 9, but decided that its sporting nature detracted from its ability to be used for self-defence. When she was 15 years old she was introduced to Ninjutsu as a complete self defence system and shortly after joined Genbukan World Ninpo Bugei. She continued training as well as exploring other martial arts such as Aikido and Kickboxing, which served to confirm her belief that Genbukan Ninpo Taijutsu is the most comprehensive system and the best one for everybody to learn.

In April 2004 she moved to Japan to study directly under Grandmaster Shoto Tanemura, where she trained in Ninpo Taijutsu, Jujutsu, Bikenjutsu (sword techniques), Iaido, Bojutsu (6ft & 3ft staff techniques) and Chugoku Kempo (Chinese martial arts) among other things.

The title Dojo-Cho and Dojo name Kijin Dojo was given to Laura by Grandmaster Tanemura Sensei, so that she could establish a place to pass on her knowledge. She currently holds 4th Dan in Ninpo Taijutsu , 4th Dan in Jujitsu, Shoden Menkyo in Mugen Shintou Ryu amongst other grades .

Training

What to expect

All classes are lead by the teacher, and all students will be given instructions as to what in particular to practice. The general format of a class is as follows:

  • Warm up
  • Ceremonial bow in
  • Basic rolls and body evasions techniques
  • Basic kicks and punches
  • Students train with a partner in techniques from their Kyu level
  • Ceremonial bow out

What to bring:

When you arrive at your first class please wear loose clothing (or a Gi) and socks. If it is hot you may wish to bring a bottle of water and a small hand towel.
